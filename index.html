The Plan

This might be the group for you if you want to meet socially conscious nerds to talk about interesting things. This is not an entrepreneurial meetup, nor is it networking: It is a support group.

  • We go to a good pub or venue.
  • We eat food.
  • We say 'computer'.
  • We drink, be it beer, cocktails or soft drinks.

There aren't regularly organized talks, workshops, or otherwise.

It's just a chance to meet good people and talk about good and bad things.

We will talk computer a lot but we'd also like to not talk computer too.

We might have a talk, we might try and learn a thing, but at the end of the day we're just here not to be alone when we find ourselves saying "computer".

Imposters Welcome

This is a support group. No-one knows what they are doing.

If you're from a background which isn't well-represented in most geeky meetups, come along – we want to make a difference.

If you're worried about not being computer enough, come.

If your day job isn't code, come.

If you think you're an imposter, come.

This isn't a group of experts, just people.

We are interested in the social and technical problems.

The Rules

A code of conduct applies wherever we gather to talk computer. This is true both in person and online.

  • Don't harass people. Don't make exclusionary jokes. Don't even make them "ironically".
  • We want to be inclusive; do not engage in homophobic, racist, transphobic, sexist, or otherwise exclusionary behavior.
  • Aggression and elitism are unwelcome here — computering is not a competition.
  • We think feminism is a good thing. Discussion of how to make computer more inclusive is welcome. Claims that this "has gone too far" aren't.
  • Although we're meeting in a pub, there is no expectation or pressure to drink alcohol. Don't question anyone's choice of drink.
  • We're a very informal and ad-hoc group right now, so if you piss us off you won't be invited again.
  • The rules will become more explicit and complete as we grow. In the meantime, anything forbidden by the Geek Feminism standard code of conduct is also forbidden here.
