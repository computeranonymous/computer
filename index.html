The Plan

This might be the group for you if you want to meet socially conscious nerds to talk about interesting things. This is not an entrepeneurial meetup, nor is it networking: It is a support group.

  • We go to a good pub or venue.
  • We eat food.
  • We say 'computer'.
  • We drink, be it beer, cocktails or soft drinks.

There aren't regularly organized talks, workshops, or otherwise.

It's just a chance to meet good people and talk about good and bad things.

We will talk computer a lot but we'd also like to not talk computer too.

We might have a talk, we might try and learn a thing, but at the end of the day we're just here not to be alone when we find ourselves saying "computer".

Imposters Welcome

This is a support group. No-one knows what they are doing.

If you're worried about not being computer enough, come along.

If you're from a background which isn't well-represented in most geeky meetups, come along – we want to make a difference.

If your day job isn't code, come along.

If you think you're an imposter, come along.

This isn't a group of experts, just people.

We are interested in the social and technical problems.

The Rules

  • Don't harass people. Don't make exclusionary jokes. Don't even make them "ironically".
  • Discriminatory behaviour (such as homophobia, racism, transphobia, or sexism) is not appropriate.
  • We think feminism is a good thing; if you disagree, this probably isn't the place for you.
  • Although we're meeting in a pub, there's no expectation or pressure to drink alcohol. Don't question anyone's choice of drink.

If you piss us off by breaking these rules, you won't be invited again. These rules may become more explicit as we grow – we're always trying to change for the better.
